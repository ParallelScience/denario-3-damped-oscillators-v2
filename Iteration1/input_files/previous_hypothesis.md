**Title**: Energy-Dissipation Rate Analysis for Robust Damping Estimation

**Hypothesis**: The current MLE approach, which relies on fitting the displacement $x(t)$, is highly sensitive to the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) at later time steps where the amplitude decays into the noise floor. I hypothesize that analyzing the time-evolution of the Total Energy $E(t) = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ provides a more robust estimation of the damping coefficient $\gamma$ than fitting the displacement directly. 

Specifically, for an underdamped oscillator, the envelope of the total energy decays as $E(t) \approx E_0 \exp(-2\gamma t)$. By performing a linear regression on the log-transformed total energy, $\ln(E(t)) \approx \ln(E_0) - 2\gamma t$, we can decouple the estimation of $\gamma$ from the oscillatory phase $\phi$ and frequency $\omega$. This method is expected to be more resilient to high-frequency measurement noise and phase-jitter, as it effectively acts as a low-pass filter by integrating kinetic and potential energy components. I propose to compare the $\gamma$ values derived from this energy-decay model against the previous MLE results to determine if this approach yields lower variance in high-damping regimes where the displacement signal is rapidly attenuated.
