**Title**: Bayesian Inference of Damping Parameters via Full-Trajectory Global Optimization

**Description**: This study proposes a Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) framework to extract damping coefficients $\gamma$ and natural frequencies $\omega$ by fitting the analytical model $x(t) = A \exp(-\gamma t) \cos(\omega t + \phi)$ directly to the 500-step trajectory of each oscillator. By utilizing the entire time-series data via non-linear least-squares minimization (`scipy.optimize.curve_fit`), we minimize the global residual sum of squares, effectively averaging out Gaussian measurement noise across the 20-second observational window. The approach incorporates a multi-start heuristic to initialize parameters based on the spectral density of the displacement field, ensuring convergence to the global optimum despite the noise floor. We quantify the estimator's covariance matrix to establish confidence intervals for the recovered $\gamma$ as a function of the signal-to-noise ratio and damping ratio. This methodology bypasses the high-frequency jitter associated with local extrema detection, providing a robust, computationally efficient mechanism for parameter recovery in underdamped systems.
